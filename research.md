My research integrates applied mathematics, computational modeling, and machine learning to study complex problems. I combine analytical and numerical methods to translate theoretical concepts into practical solutions. To support this work, I use Python and MATLAB to implement numerical methods, develop models, and analyze complex datasets, allowing me to apply both numerical and data-driven techniques effectively.

1. Mathematical Modeling and Applied Mathematics
I have a strong foundation in applied mathematics. My
research has focused on calculus of variations and numerical methods to approximate solutions. Calculus of variations is a field that studies on finding functions optimizing specifc functionals, called as energy functionals. This research is fundamental for understanding physical systems and structures, formulating them as variational problems. In particular, I studied the Skyrme energy functional, analyzing topological classes of maps called generalized twists and their relationship with the Euler–Lagrange equations. I applied advanced numerical techniques, including Fourier–Legendre spectral methods, to approximate solutions efficiently.

2. Computational Modeling and Numerical Analysis
My research also includes computational modeling using zero-dimensional (0D) lumped-parameter models to simulate complex systems. In more detail, I have worked with vessel models coupled with Windkessel terminal models to study blood flow, pressure, and volume dynamics. These models are formulated as systems of ordinary differential equations and solved using numerical methods, such as the forward Euler method. I used synthetic data to calibrate the models and estimate key parameters, applying optimization techniques to ensure accurate and reliable simulations. This work has strengthened my ability to translate theoretical concepts into computational frameworks and gain predictive insights into real-world systems.

3. Machine Learning and Data-Driven Approaches
Building on my mathematical and computational foundation, I am currently focused on bridging gap between machine learning methods and mathematics to analyze complex datasets. I am particularly interested in neural networks, especially physics-informed neural networks, applied to problems in interdisciplinary areas such as biology, healthcare, environmental science, and engineering. My goal is to develop models that are interpretable, robust, and capable of learning efficiently from structured or limited data, addressing challenges where trust and reproducibility are critical.

4. Future Directions: Bridging Theory and Applications
Looking ahead, I aim to develop frameworks that connect mathematical theory, computational methods, and machine learning to practical applications. I am motivated to create models and tools that provide more reliable predictions, and contribute to advancements in different domains. My ultimate goal is to translate abstract mathematical concepts into real-world solutions with measurable impact.
